Falcons

“The quality of decision is like the well-timed swoop of a falcon which enables it to strike and destroy its victim.”

An Overview

Falconry is the use of trained raptors for game hunting. Traditionally, the term "falcon" referred to a female bird, which were favored for hunting (due to their larger size), while the term tiercel described a male bird. The term "falcon" now applies to any bird (male or female) belonging to a species of raptor in the genus Falco.

Choice Birds

Despite the terminology, falconry is not limited to just falcons. Throughought history, many different types of raptors have been kept and trained for hunting. Over time, falconers have come to class them in four three general categories.

For more information on the different categories, as well as some less commonly used birds, visit this article about birds used in contemporary falconry on Wikipedia.org.
